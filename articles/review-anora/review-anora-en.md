# 🎬 Anora (2025) Review
## Exhausting, Chaotic, yet Absolutely Brilliant

<img width="800" alt="Anora Banner" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Daffathan-Labs/Articles/main/articles/review-anora/anora.jpg" />

This time, I want to review a movie that swept so many prestigious awards last year: *Anora*.

Honestly, after finishing this movie, the only word that perfectly describes how I felt is: **exhausted**. As an introvert, this film completely drained my energy! Overall, I'm giving it a **7/10**. Let's dive into why a movie so tiring to watch still deserves all the high praise it got.

---

## 🗣️ 1. Incredibly Loud, Chaotic, yet Natural!

The content of this movie is overwhelmingly loud and crowded! You will be thrown into scenes where multiple people are talking over each other, shouting, and arguing across different languages and cultures. The atmosphere is pure *chaos*.

But surprisingly, the chaos here isn't a bad thing. The loudness perfectly captures the raw reality of the situation. It doesn't even feel like watching people act; it feels more like a hidden camera recording real life. The acting from the entire cast deserves a standing ovation because everything feels so incredibly natural and real.

---

## 💔 2. Genuine Love vs. Transactional Love

The core premise is uniquely brutal. The movie slaps the audience with the harsh collision between transactional love and genuine love.

The character Anora desperately seeks sincerity, but reality hits her with a world that operates purely on transactions. The raw emotion she experiences is delivered flawlessly thanks to Mikey Madison's brilliant acting. It serves as a brutal take on materialism, offering valuable lessons about the harsh realities of love in the real world.

---

## ⏳ 3. Slow Pacing and Comedy Amidst the Chaos

Pacing-wise, the film is quite slow. For the first hour, we are solely treated to non-stop, over-the-top partying and spending. But hold on! The movie only truly gets interesting *after* that first hour when the main conflict finally erupts.

Once the main conflict starts, the comedy actually shines through and gets genuinely hilarious. The funny thing is, by the time those comedic moments arrived, I honestly couldn't even laugh anymore because all my energy had already been completely sucked dry by the movie's chaotic nature!

---

## 🔞 4. Vulgarity Overshadowed by the Conflict

Given the theme and background of the story, it's no surprise that this film features a lot of vulgar scenes. So, it's definitely not a movie for everyone.

However, what's interesting is that as the story progresses and the main conflict peaks, those vulgar scenes start to feel incredibly "normal." Our attention becomes entirely hijacked by the intense, chaotic scramble to find a way out, which ends up being far more engaging than the vulgarity itself.

---

## 🎬 Conclusion

*Anora* is an exceptionally chaotic, loud, and exhausting film to sit through. But that is exactly where its genius lies in capturing reality.

It is highly worth watching, provided you aren't looking for a fast-paced movie. Prepare yourself for a slow-burn experience that will drain your energy and emotions, but will undoubtedly leave a lasting impression by the end. It's easy to see why it won so many awards!

**Final Score: 7/10** ⭐️
