## Between Love, Logic, and the Reality of Marriage

I recently watched *Materialist (2025)*.

And honestly, this is not just another romantic film.  
Its message lingers.

The strongest takeaway for me connects deeply with a value often emphasized in Islam:

> **“Marry the one you love.”**

Not simply because of status.  
Not merely because of financial security.  
Not because the future looks safe on paper.

But because of love.

---

## 💍 “Marriage Is a Business”

One of Lucy’s lines that stayed with me was:

> “Marriage is a business.”

At first, it sounds cold. Rational. Almost cynical.

But as the story unfolds, I begin to understand what she truly means.

Marriage does involve:

- long-term commitment  
- shared responsibilities  
- financial stability  
- aligned life vision  

These are real and practical aspects of life.

However, Lucy makes something very clear:

> Even a business needs love inside it.

And that is what makes the film honest.  
It does not deny reality.  
But it also refuses to remove the heart from the equation.

---

## ❤️ “Love Is Easy”

Another line I deeply appreciate:

> “Love is easy.”  
> “Dating is hard because it requires effort.”

Simple words. Powerful meaning.

Love can feel natural.  
There is comfort. There is connection. There is emotional alignment.

But building a relationship?

That takes work.

I know that relationships demand:

- communication  
- consistency  
- patience  
- courage  
- real effort  

The film reinforces that pure love is not born from negotiation or strategic calculation.

Love is the foundation.

Without it, relationships can quietly turn into transactions.

---

## 💸 Wealth Without Emotional Connection

Near the end, Lucy faces a clear choice:

- A wealthy, stable man who looks “perfect” on paper.  
- A man who may not be as financially impressive, but emotionally compatible.  

And she chooses emotional compatibility.

For me, that decision carries weight.

Because in reality, society often pressures you and me to choose differently.

We are often tempted by:

- financial security  
- social status  
- outward stability  

But the film seems to whisper something important:

> Without emotional connection, even comfort can feel empty.

And once again, love becomes the central foundation.

---

## 🧠 Reflection for You and Me

After watching this film, I asked myself:

Am I looking for a partner to truly build a life together?  
Or am I just seeking security?

I understand that stability matters.  
Finances matter. Practicality matters.

But if love is not the core,  
can happiness really feel complete?

This film reminded me that pure love is actually simple.  
What complicates it are expectations and worldly calculations.

---

## 🏁 Final Thoughts

*Materialist (2025)* is not overly dramatic.  
Yet it touches on realities we often avoid.

- Marriage is practical.  
- Relationships require effort.  
- Logic has its place.  

But in the end?

> Love remains the primary reason I choose to stay.

Because when everything shifts —  
wealth can fade, status can disappear —  
what remains is feeling.

And if love was never there from the beginning,  
what were I truly building?
